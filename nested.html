Two Forgetful Uncles Hopping to the Beat

A Short Story by Kaden Baratcart

John Torrance looked at the enchanted book in his hands and felt sneezy. He walked over to the window and reflected on his hilly surroundings. He had always loved derelict Dallas with its energetic, eager estuaries. It was a place that encouraged his tendency to feel sneezy. Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Andrew Connor. Andrew was a thoughtful carer with wobbly fingers and vast legs. John gulped. He glanced at his own reflection. He was a charming, sinister, brandy drinker with dirty fingers and hairy legs. His friends saw him as a perfect, pretty painter. Once, he had even rescued a crispy puppy from a burning building. But not even a charming person who had once rescued a crispy puppy from a burning building, was prepared for what Andrew had in store today. The snow flurried like shouting mice, making John jumpy. As John stepped outside and Andrew came closer, he could see the calm smile on his face. "I am here because I want Internet access," Andrew bellowed, in a predatory tone. He slammed his fist against John's chest, with the force of 7429 bears. "I frigging love you, John Torrance." John looked back, even more jumpy and still fingering the enchanted book. "Andrew, get out of my house," he replied. They looked at each other with shocked feelings, like two hungry, happy hamsters skipping at a very cowardly Valentine's meal, which had drum and bass music playing in the background and two forgetful uncles hopping to the beat. Suddenly, Andrew lunged forward and tried to punch John in the face. Quickly, John grabbed the enchanted book and brought it down on Andrew's skull. Andrew's wobbly fingers trembled and his vast legs wobbled. He looked active, his body raw like a greasy, grotesque gun. Then he let out an agonising groan and collapsed onto the ground. Moments later Andrew Connor was dead. John Torrance went back inside and made himself a nice glass of brandy. THE END
